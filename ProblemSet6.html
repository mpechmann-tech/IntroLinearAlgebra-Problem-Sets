Problem 1 

Let 𝐮=(3,1), 𝐯=(0,−5), and 𝐰=(−3,3). Find the components of

  1. (a)

    𝐮+𝐯

  2. (b)

    3⁢(𝐮−6⁢𝐰)

  3. (c)

    2⁢𝐯−2⁢(𝐮−3⁢𝐰)

Problem 2 

Let 𝐮=(−2,3,1), 𝐯=(3,−6,−1), and 𝐰=(−0,8,4). Find the components of

  1. (a)

    𝐮−𝐰

  2. (b)

    −2⁢(𝐮+3⁢𝐰)

  3. (c)

    (2⁢𝐯−𝐮)−(3⁢𝐰+𝐮)

Problem 3

Problem 9 

Find the Euclidean distance between 𝐮 and 𝐯 and the cosine of the angle between those vectors. Also state whether that angle is acute (less than 90∘), obtuse (greater than 90∘), or 90∘.

Problem 14 

Find ‖P𝐚⁢𝐮‖ where P𝐚 is the (orthogonal) projection onto 𝐚.

  1. (a)

    𝐮=(1,2), 𝐚=(−3,4)

  2. (b)

    𝐮=(3,1,2), 𝐚=(2,3,2)

  3. (c)

    𝐮=(1,2,−1,1), 𝐚=(2,1,0,1)

Problem 15 

Decompose 𝐮 into a component parallel to 𝐚 and a component orthogonal to 𝐚.

  1. (a)

    𝐮=(3,4), 𝐚=(1,2)

  2. (b)

    𝐮=(2,−1,3), 𝐚=(1,1,1)

  3. (c)

    𝐮=(3,1,2,0), 𝐚=(1,0,1,1)

Problem 16 

Find the distance from the given point to the given line or plane:

  1. (a)

    point: (4,0), line: x−2⁢y+3=0

  2. (b)

    point: (3,1,0), plane: x+2⁢y+2⁢z−4=0

Hint: Let 𝐧 be a normal vector of a line (or a plane), and let 𝐀 be any point on the line (or on the plane). Consider the vector 𝐀𝐏=𝐏−𝐀. The distance from 𝐏 to the line (or the plane) equals the norm of the (orthogonal) projection of 𝐀𝐏 onto 𝐧.
